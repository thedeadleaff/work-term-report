Work Term 3 | S22



Digital Kitchener Innovation Lab - Smart City Developer/Data Scientist Self managed and fast paced, this workterm position offered an opportunity to work with various systems, devices, technologies and project approaches. I had worked on roughly 5 projects which involved a multitude of approaches ranging from designing a system from scratch, to picking up an old project and getting it running again after years of system updates. Presenting to higher-ups in the compay offered a great opportunity to quickly learn what is necessary to clearly communicate your point of view as well as what should be expected and possible missing pieces with plans to implement later on.
I had gained experience working with: writing code for a project on the Raspberry Pi which will deploy in late 2022/early 2023, picking up projects which code had been last updated when Python 3.6.8 had been released, backend of a website, Azure systems such as secure uploads to blob storage under a private network connection, using Power Automate to create flows which maximize the productivity for City of Kitchener staff, applications which offer cross-platform compalibility between Windows and Linux, and learning how to ensure proper documentation is completed for future developers, and updates overtime.


Goals

Thoughout the work term, many new experiences have allowed me to strengthen my development skills, communication skills, and gain new skills in an environment that was not only self managed, but also dependant on progress rather than an assigned schedule with deadlines. What this means is that this position required self-driven developers with the ability to seek solutions from several resources. Blogs were a favourite of mine as the documentation for each project was vague and lacking details, or missing altogether!



With this, my first goal in creative and critical thinking was to improve in my ability to parse through the scattered information and find solutions in a reasonable amount of time. My goal of finding the root cause of the problem was greatly tested as each project I had tackled had different documenation styles and approaches. This meant that in order to continue working and polishing the project, I had to understand what each project did, truly understand what the approach was and document my findings to help the next intern once it came time to update the system again. I have achieved this goal by not solely researching extensively, but also collaborating with my coworkers and getting an understanding of how different people would interpret each problem encountered. This was my measure of success, and I have been able to achieve it and improve on my skills by improving each project I'd work on, and documenting each success and roadblock to ensure that the next internaction/modification would be organized and easy to pick up.

Since this position counts as my first 'fully-a-developer' job, teamwork was another goal of mine that I wished to achieve and improve on. My plan of action was to ensure that all and any progress as recorded in someway, and communication as the foundation for any and all projects during the term. This was acheived in such a small window of time thanks to the position being in-person. Having your boss and coworkers a chair spin away greatly improved the response and testing times for each success and fix as we were all able to show our understanding and thinking verbally in conjunction to the working technology. Looking back, I found that my goal of being an asset to my team has been achieved as I was able to refine my social skills in a way that allows everyone to my understand complex thought processes, ideas and document the good, bad and awesome so that the future of the project can be picked up and updated as smoothly as possible.

Connected to goal #2, my goal to communicate my ideas in a comprehensive and organized manner is also well worked on during this term. Oral communication has been a semi-struggle for me previously as I found that my thought process and the way my brain parses the info I'm internalizing is busy (some would say hectic!); like a spider web that connects many ideas that wouldn't be seen as related. Again, having this position in-person is what I believe helped me achieve this goal in such a narrow timeframe, since my plan of action required some form of explanation that online doesn't offer. What doesn't online offer? My plan of action was to use various tools around me to organize my thoughts and ideas in a way that would show the connections without being overwhelming. I also added to my goal to take my time thinking about how I was going to reverse engineer my thoughts and reorder them to explain it in a linear fashion. This goal was successful since I was able to get some of the most out-there ideas understood by using sketches, other visuals, and keeping the user and other developers in mind as I'd work on each project.

Work Term 1 | S21

Animal Biosciences - Shackle Turkey Imaging

First work term, but what is the role? The position itself involved precise photo editing skills, a strong attention to detail, and profound patience. Initially, the role sounds dull and repetative, unlike it's impact on the final product. Shackle Turkey Imaging consisted of a series of videos shackle turkeys after slaughter. Some of these turkeys has zip ties. These zipties signified a greater solution; to use technology to accurately measure the quality and weight of meat.

I had experience in photo editing using Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator and in drawing programs such as FireAlpaca and Clip Studio Paint. Using these skills, I was able to use VLC Media Player to capure .tiff snapshots of the passing turkeys, and remove the backgrounds using a photo editing program. Using Clip Studio Paint proved to be the most efficient method for this as keybinds were easy to implement, and the responsiveness of the application saved even more time. CSP and Photoshop's results were extreamly similar, and the results produced were identical. Since my primary computing device is my laptop (a Macbook), CSP was not only speedy but was also accessible from anywhere I could bring my laptop! That meant if the opportunity presented itself, I could work anywhere, not just in a home office.

Out of these 5 goals, the main improvements I wanted to make were communicating, creativity and personal growth. I believe I've achieved these goals over my work term, and will continue to improve as I succeed in other work terms. Hence my first goal; to find new ways of completing work. I became condifent in my ability to make my work as efficient as possible by taking advantage of the fact that this work was completed on a computer. By trying to ensure I was communicating well while working, it would force me to go out of my comfort zone and, in turn, be a better employee as the work done would meet every requirement.

All in all, I feel as though I was able to complete my goals with the ability to continue with these goals for my next position. I was able to fulfill each goal adequately. I was able to communicate with my boss and co-worker, but can still work on my deliveries.
